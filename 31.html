Family is just such a big thing within Chinese culture, there’s such a huge emphasis on it, and there’s been a lot of challenges within my familial relationships. Learning to prioritize, balance my independence and my personal interests with my family—I noticed that’s sort of a struggle within Chinese culture.

I think growing up Chinese, you see that Chinese people have different ways of communicating. It’s less straightforward sometimes where they don’t say things as bluntly or as upfront, I noticed. So I try and be really communicative, and that I’m getting the point across. So sometimes that means I’m taking different methods than my parents. Like I’m just being really frank, saying whatever that comes up to my mind, or I’m talking back. Balancing those things has just been a learning process for me.

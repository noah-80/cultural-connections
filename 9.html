Oh, definitely, for sure. Sometimes I say my dad is more ABC“American-born Chinese,” those who are ethnically Chinese but born and raised American. than I am. I think I see my grandparents on my mom’s side a lot more because they live in California, and my dad’s side, they still live in Minnesota. With my mom’s parents, my maternal grandparents, they’re kind of the picture of Chinese grandparents. They’re always making buns and giving them to us. Or whenever we visit, they’re giving us 红包A red envelope (红包) is a gift of money, an act of wishing good fortune.. So I would say that is how I feel kind of rooted to my Chinese culture versus on my dad’s side. It’s mainly through my grandparents rather than my dad.
